## How to read this

The newsroom is **topic-agnostic**: a use case is just a set of **beats** plus
the **specialist roles** that serve them, under the same values (human-in-the-
loop, sourced, fair pay, editorial independence). Below, the two lead examples
are detailed; others are sketched. None of these publish autonomously — each
produces a sourced briefing a human turns into action or a story.

> **Important framing:** the service does **research and curation**, not advice.
> For regulated domains (property, finance, legal) it surfaces sourced
> information for a human professional to act on — it is not financial, legal, or
> investment advice.

## 1. Charity / non-profit — competitive campaign intelligence

**Who it's for:** fundraising and campaign teams, communications leads, grant
writers.

**The problem:** charities compete for attention and donations but rarely have
time to track what every other cause in their space is doing, where funding is
moving, or which messages are landing.

**Beats:**
- Competing campaigns and appeals in your cause area (launches, themes, results).
- Grant and funding opportunities; major gifts and corporate partnerships.
- Donor and giving trends; sector reports and benchmarks.
- Regulatory / tax changes affecting charities and giving.
- Relevant news your cause should respond to quickly.

**Specialists that help:**
- **Research specialists** — sector reports, foundation/registry data, primary
  documents.
- **News-hounds** — rising causes, crises, or moments to respond to in time.
- **Sourcing specialists** — sector experts and journalists for comment.

**Value:** position campaigns competitively, time launches around the field,
spot un-served angles ("white space"), and react to news while it's live —
without a person scanning all day.

**Watch-outs:** accuracy and sensitivity matter in this sector; verify before
acting; never misrepresent another organisation.

**A morning brief might lead with:** *"[Official] National peer launches winter
appeal — emotive, matched-giving; [high] new federal grant stream opens Friday;
[medium] giving-trends report shows under-35 donors shifting to recurring gifts."*

## 2. Real estate — market research

**Who it's for:** agents, brokerages, investors, developers, mortgage advisers.

**The problem:** the market moves daily — listings, rates, zoning, new
developments — and staying ahead is a full-time scan.

**Beats:**
- Local listings and price movements; inventory and days-on-market shifts.
- Mortgage rates and lending changes.
- Zoning, planning, and policy decisions; permits and approvals.
- New developments and major projects nearby.
- Competitor brokerages/agents and their activity.
- Neighbourhood and local economic indicators.

**Specialists that help:**
- **Research specialists** — planning documents, market/economic data, primary
  filings.
- **News-hounds** — early signals of a development or policy shift.
- **Rights & licensing** — clearing imagery/maps for use.
- **Translation & accessibility** — multilingual, accessible market briefs.

**Value:** a daily edge — walk into client meetings already briefed, spot
opportunities and risks early, and back positioning with sourced data.

**Watch-outs:** research, not advice; cite sources; respect listing/data
licensing and fair housing/advertising rules.

**An afternoon brief might lead with:** *"[high] Council approves rezoning on
Mill St — density up, 200+ units; [high] lender drops 5-yr fixed 25bps; [medium]
competitor opens a second office in the east end."*

## 3. Other applications (sketches)

- **Competitive / market intelligence (B2B).** Track rivals, product launches,
  pricing, partnerships, and sector moves across your industry.
- **Policy & regulatory watch (GRC).** Government action, consultations,
  standards, enforcement — natural fit alongside Persona-x / Brightpath work.
- **Personal brand / thought leadership.** A daily feed of what to comment on in
  your field, with sources, so you publish faster and smarter.
- **Local journalism / community.** Hyper-local coverage, with paid local
  contributors, translation, and accessibility built in.
- **Investor / sector watch.** Company, sector, and macro signals curated for a
  human analyst (research, not investment advice).
- **Events & conferences.** Track a fast-moving event or launch window with a
  short-window, high-frequency beat.

## 4. Turning a use case on

Each use case is configured, not rebuilt:
1. Define the **beats** (the topics above) as reporter prompts.
2. Set the **recency window** and **significance bar** to suit the domain
   (fast markets → tight window; slow sectors → wider).
3. Add any **specialist roles** the use case needs (research, sourcing,
   news-hound, rights, translation, accessibility).
4. Keep the **human-in-the-loop** gate and the shared values in place.

The editor, dashboard, editions, and governance are unchanged — only the beats
and dials move.
